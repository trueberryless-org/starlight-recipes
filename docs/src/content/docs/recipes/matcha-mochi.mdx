  description: "Matcha mochi is a delightful fusion of chewy and sweet textures with the earthy, slightly bitter flavor of matcha green tea. This beloved Japanese treat combines the unique stickiness of mochi with the aromatic taste of matcha, creating a dessert that’s both indulgent and wholesome. Whether you’re enjoying it as a midday snack or sharing it with friends over tea, matcha mochi is sure to impress with its vibrant green hue and tender bite.\n\nThis vegan version of matcha mochi uses coconut milk and vegan butter to achieve its characteristic creamy texture, making it a perfect dessert for plant-based eaters and mochi lovers alike. With just a handful of simple ingredients, you can whip up a batch of this irresistible sweet treat.\n\nIngredients:\n\n2 cups glutinous rice flour\n3/4 cup caster sugar\n1 tablespoon matcha powder + 1 teaspoon for dusting\n1 teaspoon baking powder\n1.5 cups coconut milk\n1/4 cup vegan butter\n1 teaspoon vanilla extract\n\nInstructions:\n\nIn a large mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, caster sugar, baking powder, and matcha powder. Mix well with a whisk.\n\nAdd the coconut milk, vegan butter, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Whisk thoroughly until the batter is smooth, thick, and flowy, as shown in the video.\n\nPour the batter into a parchment-lined baking pan and spread it evenly.\n\nBake in a preheated oven at 160°C (320°F) for 40 minutes, or until fully set.\n\nRemove the baked mochi from the oven and let it rest for 10–15 minutes.\n\nTransfer the mochi to a chopping board and cut it into squares. Allow it to cool further if needed.\n\nUsing a strainer, dust the squares with matcha powder."
